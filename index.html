Challenge

In Kenya, and in other countries in Africa, the lives of more than 3000 mothers, grandmothers, daughters and wives are cut short every year by cervical cancer and this number is meant to grow by 2025.
Lack of awareness and accessibility of cervical cancer screening in rural areas is to date the biggest challenge: only 3% of women in Kenya take part in cervical cancer screening. For their sisters around the world this number is 70%.  Why this huge inequality? Fear, economic uncertainty, stigma and misinformation in the society also play a hurdle in accessing services and treatments even when they are available.
But we also have good news ! Eradicating this disease within our lifetime is indeed possible. Let's work together now to save the lives of millions of women around the world.

Solution

We would like to hold the hand of women and their families and assist them during their cervical cancer prevention journey. 
This journey starts by  providing education to Community Health Volunteers (CHVs) on sexual health topics and cervical cancer disease and prevention. CHVs will then have the knowledge and tools to raise awareness about the disease in the rural communities of the Bungoma county in Kenya.

We would like to hold the hand of women and their families and assist them during their cervical cancer prevention journey. CHVs will also play a crucial role in identifying  our patients (women of reproductive age)  and linking  them to local health clinics where they can have access to the cervical cancer screening service. Furthermore, once women receive their diagnosis,  we will provide assistance in managing their follow-up treatments by working closely with our local partner Academic Model Providing Access to Care (AMPATH) and the ministry of health.

Our theory of change

An informed change of direction

It is s only  when women and their families understand the importance of disease prevention and of how regular access to  this service can save lives of thousands of women in their communities, that they will develop a long-lasting health-seeking behaviour. By combining sexual health education, cervical cancer awareness and access to healthcare, we empower women in the society to take charge of their own health and to take informed decisions about their wellbeing in the future.

Our Mission

Our Mission

We listen to the needs of local women and their families. We work with them to bring solutions that they can identify with. We trigger a health-seeking behaviour and economic empowerment in rural communities that will allow for sustainable solutions while contributing to an ecosystem of change.

Our Vision

We envisage a world where equal access to quality health education, affordable and sustainable healthcare will eradicate curable and manageable diseases and create a positive impact on the lives of many families in the rural communities of western Kenya.

Our Impact

Our work will have a positive impact on the lives of women and their families in the Bungoma County in Kenya. The villages of Webuye East, Bungoma North and Kanduyi will be our primary focus.
